I never imagined I'd end up climbing mountains, much less Heimi Peak. Slowly, I set sail for places further away, intoxicated by the joy of exploration. I can, without any outside help, measure the world with my own two feet. On my first few trips up the mountain, the sky was overcast and a fine rain hung in the air. ![](IMG_6852-scaled-e1618031997867-768x1024.jpg) ![](IMG_6979-1024x768.jpg) I think back to the memory of climbing alone through the fog — drifting like a wandering immortal in the mist, not knowing what lay ahead, not knowing the way back either. I roamed in the cloud, lost in white emptiness, and the feeling was so empty and so lonely. ![](IMG_6978-1024x768.jpg) I've been to Heimi Peak many times, with friends, with my father, alone. Each visit has its own gifts, and most of all the feeling of being alive. Every time I drag myself home exhausted, I notice how lucky I am. Moving forward slowly takes a kind of stubborn resolve. ![](IMG_6920-1024x768.jpg) ![](IMG_7184-1024x1017.jpg) But that wasn't the end of the trip. Gao Ruiling discovered a steep path that seemed to climb even higher into the mountain. A sign mentioned an old temple further up. We pushed our bikes up the slope, and suddenly realized this stretch was much longer and much steeper than we'd thought. Past 45° there's only one option: push. By the time we reached the real summit I was completely spent — my legs wouldn't lift off the saddle. I sat still for a quarter of an hour. Up here, apart from the meteorological station, nothing was higher than us. Finally I pulled out my snacks: chips and Sprite, the supreme indulgence at the top of the mountain. The blue of the sky was that pure, and in that moment all I could feel was the numbness in my legs, the crispness of the chips, and the warmth of the sun on my skin. Everything else felt dim. Life is exactly that simple — the sun rises every day, chips and Sprite are within easy reach, and yet we constantly overlook them, hurrying through every street and alley in pursuit of a few extra fragments of joy. ![](IMG_7196-1024x768.jpg)
